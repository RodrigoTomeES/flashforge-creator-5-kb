# Extruder Box Debris Cleaning

Source: https://wiki.flashforge.com/en/creator-ser/creator-5m-series/maintenance/mtc2

> During the long-term use of the extruder, the mutual extrusion and friction between the consumables will cause the consumables to shed debris on the extruder, deceleration wheel, and loosening wheel. Over time, the consumables debris will fill the extruder box and gear gaps, increase the resistance of gear rotation, cause slipping during wire feeding, and accelerate the wear of gears and gear shafts. Therefore, regular cleaning of extruder components helps to ensure stable wire feeding and improve the life of the extruder.

**1.Maintenance Cycle**

Every 3000 hours of printing

# Clean and Lubricate the X-Axis Linear Shaft 

Source: https://wiki.flashforge.com/en/creator-series/creator-5-series/maintenance/clean-and-lubricate-the-x-axis-linear-shaft

> During long-term operation, the X-Axis Linear Shaft may accumulate some dust due to friction and the intrusion of external dust, which can cause the Y axes to run smoothly and become stuck during the operation of the print head. Slight issues may affect the appearance of the printed model, while severe ones may lead to laggy printing and offset.

**1、Maintenance Cycle**

High-frequency usage (average daily printing time ≥ 5 hours): Perform a full cleaning and lubrication of the X axis once a month.  
Regular usage (average daily printing time 1–5 hours): Perform maintenance of the X axis once every 2 months.  
Low-frequency usage (average daily printing time < 1 hour): Perform maintenance of the X axis once every 3 months.

**2、Maintenance Tools**

WD-40 High-Efficiency Silicone Lubricant

Cleanroom paper/Cleanroom cloth

# Cooling Fan Dust Cleaning

Source: https://wiki.flashforge.com/en/creator-series/creator-5-series/maintenance/cooling-fan-dust-cleaning

> During long-term use, dust will gradually accumulate on the blades of the cooling fan, and as time passes, the dust will become thicker and thicker. This will cause the fan's air volume to decrease, resulting in insufficient heat dissipation of the model, and ultimately leading to a decline in printing quality.

**1、Maintenance Cycle**

If the average daily working hours T < 5 hours, it is recommended to clean the equipment once every 9 months after activation;

If the average daily working hours are 5 hours < T < 10 hours, it is recommended to clean the equipment once every 6 months after activation;

If the average daily working hours T > 10 hours, it is recommended to clean the equipment once every 3 months after activation;

# Feed Motor Gear Debris Cleaning

Source: https://wiki.flashforge.com/en/creator-series/creator-5-series/maintenance/feed-motor-gear-debris-cleaning

> During long-term use of the extruder, due to the mutual extrusion and friction with the consumables, the consumables will shed debris inside the extruder, on the reduction wheel, and on the idler wheel. Over time, the consumable debris will fill the extruder box and gear gaps, increasing the resistance to gear rotation, causing slippage during filament feeding, and accelerating the wear of gears and gear shafts. Therefore, regularly cleaning the extruder components helps ensure the stability of filament extrusion and extends the lifespan of the extruder.

# Nozzle Cold Pull Cleaning

Source: https://wiki.flashforge.com/en/creator-series/creator-5-series/maintenance/nozzle-cold-pull-cleaning

> During long-term use at high temperatures, the nozzle will gradually carbonize the filament adhering to its surface, causing the diameter of the extruded filament to become smaller or even uneven; alternatively, due to the use of poor-quality consumables, internal impurities may partially block the nozzle. To improve the nozzle's service life and printing success rate, it is particularly important that we regularly perform cold pull cleaning on the nozzle.

**1、Cleaning Frequency**

-   Every 400 cumulative hours of printing；
-   Or the diameter of the outgoing wire is less than 0.45mm, with a normal diameter of approximately 0.5±0.03mm;

# Z-axis Linear Bearing Cleaning & Lubrication

Source: https://wiki.flashforge.com/en/creator-series/creator-5-series/maintenance/z-axis-linear-bearing-cleaning-and-lubrication

> During long-term operation, the optical axis of the Z-axis may experience some particle crystallization on the guide rail due to friction and the intrusion of external dust. This can cause the Z-axis to run smoothly and become laggy during the operation of the print head. Slight issues may lead to the appearance or exacerbation of Z-direction layer lines in the printed model, while severe cases can cause lag and abnormal platform operation.

**1、Maintenance Cycle**

Depending on the usage frequency, we recommend the following maintenance intervals:

High-frequency usage (average daily printing time ≥ 5 hours): perform deep maintenance of the Z axis once every 3 months.  
Regular usage (average daily printing time 1–5 hours):perform maintenance of the Z axis once every 4 months.  
Low-frequency usage (average daily printing time < 1 hour): perform maintenance of the Z axis once every 5 months.

**2、Maintenance Tools**

WD-40 High-Efficiency Silicone Lubricant

Cleanroom Paper/Cleanroom Cloth
